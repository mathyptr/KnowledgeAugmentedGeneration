Jay Chou, born on January 18, 1979, in New Taipei City, Taiwan, with ancestral roots in Yongchun County, Fujian Province, is a Mandopop singer, musician, actor, director, and screenwriter. He graduated from Tamkang High School.

In 2000, he released his debut album, *Jay*. In 2001, his album *Fantasy* established his style, blending Eastern and Western music. In 2002, he held his "The One" world tour. In 2003, he appeared on the cover of *Time* magazine; that same year, he released the album *Ye Huimei*, which won the Best Pop Vocal Album award at the 15th Golden Melody Awards. In 2004, he released the album *Seven Mile Fragrance*, which sold 3 million copies in its first month in Asia; that same year, he won the World Music Award for Best-Selling Artist in China. In 2005, he starred in his first film, *Initial D*, for which he won the Best New Performer awards at the 25th Hong Kong Film Awards and the 42nd Golden Horse Awards. From 2006 onwards, he won the World Music Award for Best-Selling Artist in China for three consecutive years.

In 2007, he wrote and directed the romance film *Secret*, and in the same year, he founded JVR Music. Also in 2007, he won the Best Composer Award at the 19th Golden Melody Awards for his song "Blue and White Porcelain". In 2007, he was selected as one of CNN's "25 Most Influential People in Asia"; in the same year, he won the Best Mandarin Male Singer Award at the 20th Golden Melody Awards for his album *Capricorn*. In 2010, he was named one of *Fast Company*'s "100 Most Creative People in the World". In 2011, he won the Best Mandarin Male Singer Award at the 22nd Golden Melody Awards for his album *The Era*.
