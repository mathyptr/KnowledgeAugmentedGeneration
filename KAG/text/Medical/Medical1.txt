KNOWLEDGE GRAPH STRUCTURE AND REASONING RULES

1. ENTITY DEFINITIONS
- MedicalKG.Drug: Represents the drug's brand name ('brandName' property). Each drug is linked to an active ingredient via the 'contains' relationship.
- MedicalKG.Ingredient: Represents the active ingredient (name). Contains the biochemical properties and interactions.
- MedicalKG.Disease: Represents the pathology (e.g., ulcer).

2. RELATIONSHIP LOGIC (MULTI-HOP REASONING)
The system must navigate the relationships between nodes to answer:
- Interactions: To find interactions between two products (Drugs), identify the respective active ingredients (Ingredients) via the 'contains' relationship and check for the presence of interaction relationships between them.
- Contraindications: A drug is contraindicated for a disease if its ingredient has a 'contraindicatedFor' relationship to that Disease node.
- Synonyms and Brand: The 'brandName' and 'name' properties are interchangeable. If the user requests a brand name (e.g., Coumadin), the system must map the search to the corresponding active ingredient (e.g., Warfarin).

3. BIOLOGICAL REASONING
For biological explanation, the system must extract information from the 'biological_reason', 'mechanism', or 'description' properties associated with the Ingredient nodes or their relationships.
